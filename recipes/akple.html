Akple (Fermented Corn & Cassava Dough Dumpling)

Description

Akple is a traditional Ghanaian staple, primarily associated with the Ewe people. Made from a blend of fermented corn and cassava dough, it's typically served with okra soup or ground pepper and fish. Soft, smooth, and filling, it's a core part of southern Ghanaian cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fermented corn dough
  • 1 cup cassava dough
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Mix the corn and cassava dough together in a large saucepan.
  2. Add water gradually while stirring to form a smooth, lump-free mixture.
  3. Place the mixture over medium heat. Stir continuously with a wooden spatula (ta).
  4. As it thickens, add a little water, cover, and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  5. Stir again vigorously to ensure it's well-cooked and stretchy.
  6. Mold into balls using a bowl and serve hot with okra soup, ground pepper, or fish stew.
